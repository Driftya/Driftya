Driftya Blog Cover Image Style Guide
1. Core Visual Theme

Every cover image should communicate quiet human interaction in a calm environment.

The imagery should feel:

• reflective
• intimate
• calm rather than dramatic
• slightly magical but grounded in reality

Scenes should show people interacting with messages, thoughts, or each other, not performing for an audience.

Examples from your images:

giving a letter to someone

sitting in a circle sharing something

quietly looking at a message

messages drifting between people

The visual metaphor should always be one message moving between people.

2. Art Style
Painterly Digital Illustration

The art style should be:

• soft painterly brush strokes
• semi-realistic characters
• warm lighting
• soft depth of field
• subtle glow effects

Avoid:

• sharp vector styles
• cartoon exaggeration
• hyperrealistic photography
• noisy textures

Lighting should usually be:

• sunset
• sunrise
• blue hour
• early night

These times naturally create the calm color palettes seen in your images.

3. Composition
Human Focus

People should always be present.

Recommended patterns:

Two people

exchanging a message

sitting across from each other

quietly interacting

Small group (3–6 people)

sitting in a circle

sharing something central

listening rather than performing

Avoid:

• crowds
• audiences
• stages
• influencer-like poses

People should feel like participants, not performers.

4. Character Design

Characters should look like ordinary young people, not stylized influencers.

Typical appearance:

• casual clothing (hoodies, sweaters, jackets)
• backpacks
• simple hairstyles
• glasses sometimes
• neutral expressions

Expressions should feel:

• thoughtful
• slightly shy
• calm
• curious

Avoid:

• exaggerated anime expressions
• fashion model poses
• dramatic emotions

5. Clothing Color System (Mood Language)

Clothing color communicates emotional tone.

Use colors consistently.

Mood	Clothing Color	Meaning
Soft	warm beige / light orange	comfort, warmth
Quiet	muted blue / gray	calm thinking
Bright	warm yellow	optimism, clarity
Heavy	muted violet / purple	introspection
Restless	cool gray / teal	uncertainty

Example:

the yellow shirt in your images becomes the emotional anchor

blue hoodies represent quiet reflection

orange clothing represents warmth

Clothing should always be matte and slightly desaturated.

6. Color Palette

The overall palette should match Driftya's calm aesthetic.

Primary tones in backgrounds:

• sunset peach
• lavender sky
• dusk blue
• warm gold highlights
• soft violet shadows

Approximate palette:

Sunset Gold      #f4b860
Lavender Sky     #9fa8ff
Soft Peach       #f5a7a1
Calm Blue        #6d86b3
Warm Violet      #8c78a5
Night Blue       #394c73

Lighting highlights should often be gold or soft white glow.

7. Message Visualization

Messages should feel gentle and physical, not like UI screenshots.

Good visual metaphors:

• glowing letter
• floating speech bubble
• drifting message cards
• glowing circle connection
• message traveling through the air

Avoid:

• visible app interfaces
• obvious UI elements
• big tech-looking overlays

Messages should look almost magical but subtle.

8. Environment

Locations should feel calm and familiar.

Recommended settings:

• lakeside at sunset
• quiet campus
• park at dusk
• rooftop overlooking city
• quiet forest clearing

Nature or open spaces work best.

Avoid:

• busy streets
• crowded interiors
• loud urban environments

9. Lighting

Lighting should be soft and atmospheric.

Typical lighting setups:

Golden hour

warm highlights

long shadows

Twilight

violet sky

soft glow

Night

moonlight + warm lamps

Always maintain soft contrast.

10. Symbolic Motifs

Recurring motifs help build visual identity.

Examples you already use:

• glowing message
• circular connection ring
• drifting messages
• letter passing hands
• calm water reflections

These should appear subtly.

11. Framing

Recommended formats:

Blog hero: 16:9

Safe composition:

• people slightly off-center
• horizon line low
• sky used for breathing space

Avoid tight crops.

12. Emotional Tone

Every image should feel like:

a small quiet moment between people.

Not:

• viral content
• attention grabbing
• dramatic storytelling

The viewer should feel:

"This moment belongs to these people."

13. Visual Consistency Rules

Always include:

✓ people
✓ calm lighting
✓ sunset/dusk palette
✓ subtle message metaphor

Never include:

✗ loud graphics
✗ high contrast neon colors
✗ influencer posing
✗ visible phone apps or feeds

You can reuse this:
